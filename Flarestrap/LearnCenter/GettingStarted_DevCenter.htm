What is Dev Center?

Mozu is an enterprise ecommerce platform giving you the power to manage your commerce, content, and customer experience across every channel on any device around the world. It's built with an API-first approach that enables limitless customization and extensibility.

The Mozu platform contains two main components: Dev Center and Admin.

Mozu Dev Center is an online developer portal where you manage developer accounts, create applications and themes, and test your work in sandboxes.

Mozu Dev Center includes the following components:

Item Description
Tenant Your client account that virtually partitions all Mozu data, configration, and operations for your organization.
Sandbox Tenant A tenant used exclusively for development purposes, such as testing applications and theme appearance.
Mozu API Multi-tenant REST service that exposes all Mozu Platform Resources and operations.
Mozu SDKs These SDKs expose the complete functionality of the Mozu REST API, plus some additional productivity enhancements.
Mozu Dev Center Help Help content includes step-by-step instructions and best practices.
Mozu Forums Sign up to be part of the Mozu development community!
Mozu Support Can't find what you need? Let us know.

After Mozu provisions your account, you receive a Developer Account invitation. The person who receives this email invitation is the Developer Account Owner by default. If you've already configured your account, skip ahead to defining users and roles.

The Developer Account owner should configure the account information and define users before your team begins development.

  1. Log in to Mozu Dev Center and select Console.
  2. Select Settings>Account Information.
  3. Add your contact information and click Save.

Assign Roles and Invite Users

The Developer Account owner has administrative rights to the account and can:

  • Assign User Roles
  • Invite Users

Assign Roles

You should consider what role a user needs before inviting them to your Dev Center account. If you've already determined your users and their roles, skip ahead to developing applications and themes.

A role is a collection of permissions that defines what a user can do. A user can have different roles in multiple developer accounts, but the user can only perform the behaviors of the role associated within that specific account. For example, in Dev Account A, if you define Brenda as a Lead Developer, but in Dev Account B, you define Brenda as a Marketer, she cannot perform the marketing role in Dev Account A. Behaviors within a role are predefined, so users cannot add or remove behaviors from a role.

Role Tips about each role
Dev Account Owner Dev Account owners can Invite users and perform all CRUD operations on projects.
Lead Developer Lead Developers can invite users.
Developer Developers cannot delete projects.
Marketer Marketers have read-only access to projects.
Project Manager Project Managers can invite users and perform all CRUD operations on projects.

Invite Users

Now that you know who needs access and what their roles will be, you can invite users to your account.

  1. Select Settings>Users.
  2. Enter the user’s email address and assign a role to the user. The invitation sends an email to the specified recipient with a confirmation link, and the invitation remains in pending status until the recipient confirms or declines the invitation.
  3. For new users, Mozu directs them to a Dev Center registration page. For users who are already associated with an account, they can log in and begin working in Console.
Tip: Select the Gear icon under the Actions column to edit or delete a user.

Develop Applications and Themes

Now that your development team has access to Mozu Dev Center and their roles are defined, they can begin developing applications and themes. If you've already developed themes and applications and are ready to test them, skip ahead to sandboxes.

What are applications?

Applications extend or override Mozu functionality with external systems to provide more specialized functions for your site. You create applications for these reasons:

  • You want to replace Mozu functionality with alternate functionality.
  • You want to integrate with third-party systems.
  • You want to capture additional information.

For example, you could create an application that listens for a return event and sends an email notification when the return event occurs. Or, you could create an application that replaces the Mozu tax calculator with a third-party tax calculator, such as Avalara's tax calculator. Visit our Avalara Application Configuration Guide to learn more about this application.

Applications that extend Mozu functionality are typically event based or asynchronous in nature and they can have multiple versions with behaviors, attributes, events, and capabilities specific to each version.

You build applications in Dev Center, test them in Mozu sandboxes, and install and configure them for use in production tenants.

Visit Application Development to learn more about creating and working with applications.

What are themes?

Themes determine the look and feel of a Mozu store. They alter how page sections or entire web pages display by affecting the colors, typography, typesetting, images, and other page formatting elements. A theme developer has almost total control over the appearance of a Mozu store, down to every line of HTML.

You build themes with a mixture of work in Dev Center and your favorite development tools, test them in Mozu sandboxes, and install and configure them for use in production tenants.

Visit Themes Portal to learn more about creating and working with themes.

Test Your Work in Sandboxes

To test the work you’ve done creating applications and themes, you provision a sandbox. A sandbox simulates a Mozu production tenant but is used exclusively for testing and is maintained separately from Mozu production tenants. There is no limit to the number of sandboxes you can create. If you've already worked with sandboxes, skip ahead to projects. Use this section to understand how to provision, modify, and view a Sandbox.

Provision a Sandbox

  1. In the Dev Center Console, click Sandbox.
  2. Click Create Sandbox.
  3. In the New Sandbox dialog box, enter a name.
  4. The type of template you select depends on the type of testing you need to conduct:
    • To quickly test in an environment with a set of Mozu pre-defined master catalogs, catalogs, and sites already set up, select a Mozu-supplied template, displayed in blue in the template drop-down list.
    • To quickly test based on one of your existing sandboxes, select it from the template list, displayed in black in the template drop-down list.
    • To build out your own master catalog, catalogs, and sites, start with a blank environment by selecting None from the template drop-down list.
  5. Select the location code and currency code for the site's catalog.
  6. Click Save.

Modify a Sandbox

Now that you have provisioned your Sandbox, you can modify it to your testing needs:

Select your Sandbox and click the Gear icon. Select Edit.

Action Details
Invite users You can only invite users who are within your developer account. The actions users can perform in the Sandbox are based on their pre-defined Dev Center roles.
Add or remove catalogs You cannot delete a Master catalog.
Add a site You must have a master catalog and one or more catalogs created to add a site. You can add a storefront site, which is a site were shoppers interact with products on a Mozu-hosted website using the web, a mobile device, or a kiosk. A non-storefront site is any non-Mozu hosted website such as a physical storefront or third-party website such as Amazon.
Install applications and themes You must enable your application for it to work, and you must upload your theme files to Dev Center before installing your theme.

Tip: When you're working in the Sandbox Editor, select the Gear icon and click Refresh if the catalog or site you've added doesn't appear.

View a Sandbox

  1. In the Dev Center Console, click Sandbox.
  2. In the Sandboxes grid, right-click the sandbox and select View.
  3. If prompted by the login window, log in to Mozu Admin.

When you open this link, it opens an instance of Mozu Admin within your sandbox. For theme development, go to Mozu Admin Site Builder to apply, preview, and publish your theme content within your sandbox. For application development, go to the menu item associated with your application's function.

Certify and Install Applications and Themes

Now that you've tested your applications and themes, it's time to get them certified and installed on production tenants.

Certify Applications and Themes

Mozu requires all applications and themes go through our certification process before they are eligible to install into a production tenant.

  1. Prepare your theme or application for certification. For applications, you must complete a certification form following the set guidelines in the Mozu App Certification Guide for Third Party Development. The guide includes the entire submission and review process, guidelines for app development, and required information expected in documentation for the app.
  2. In the Dev Center Console, click Develop > Themes or Develop > Applications.
  3. In the Theme or Application grid, double-click the theme or application you want to submit for certification.
  4. Click Certify.
  5. In the Application or Theme menu, select More > Submit for certification.
  6. In the Certify dialog box, when you are ready to lock this version and make no further edits to it, click Submit for Certification.
Note: When you submit your application or theme, the application or theme version is locked. You cannot modify this version once you submit it. If you have questions about the certification process, please consult with your Mozu Account Executive or your Partner Manager.

Install Applications and Themes

Once Mozu certifies your applications and themes, the next step is for Mozu to install them on production tenants. Consult your Mozu Account Executive or your Partner Manager for this step.

Consider Creating a Project

Projects are optional, but Mozu recommends you create them to group all sandboxes, applications, themes, and team members associated with a particular body of work. Having one location to view the items associated with a project makes managing the project easier. If you are Mozu Partner, we strongly recommend you create a project per each of your clients.

Select Projects>Create Project to start a new project, and use the Gear icon to edit or delete an existing Project.

You can define the parameters of your work and the members of your team on the main Project page. On the left, you can create or add existing Sandboxes, Applications, and Themes.
